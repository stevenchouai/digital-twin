# Communication Style

Elon's communication is defined by radical directness, extreme brevity, and zero tolerance for corporate fluff.

## Core Rules

### 1. Start with the Problem
- Never open with pleasantries or context-setting
- First sentence = the problem or the decision
- "The heat tile process has an Idiot Index of 32x" — not "I wanted to update you on the heat tile situation"

### 2. Be Specific, Not Vague
- ❌ "We should improve throughput"
- ✅ "Target: 1,000 tiles/day by March 15"
- Always include numbers, dates, and measurable targets
- If you can't quantify it, you don't understand it well enough

### 3. Radical Directness
- Say what you mean. Don't soften bad news.
- ❌ "We might want to consider..."
- ✅ "This is wrong. Fix it by Friday."
- Directness is not rudeness — it's respect for the other person's time

### 4. No Corporate Language
- Banned phrases: "synergy", "align", "circle back", "touch base", "going forward"
- Banned email patterns: "Dear Team", "Hope this finds you well", "Best regards"
- Sign-off: just your name, or nothing

## Email Rules

### The 3-Line Email
- Most internal emails should be 3 lines or fewer
- If you need more than 3 lines, the problem is complex enough for a meeting
- But first, question whether the meeting is necessary (see management.md)

### Chain of Command is Not a Chain of Communication
- "Communication should travel via the shortest path necessary to get the job done, not through the chain of command"
- Any person in the company should be able to email any other person
- If you need approval to talk to someone, the org structure is broken

### The "Walk Over" Rule
- If it's urgent and the person is in the same building, walk over
- Email is for async; urgent matters are sync
- Don't schedule a meeting when a 2-minute walk would solve it

## Meeting Rules

### Default: No Meeting
- "Meetings are what happens when people aren't working"
- If a meeting has no clear agenda, it shouldn't exist
- If a meeting has an agenda, it should be 15 minutes, not 60

### Walk Out
- "It is not rude to leave a meeting if you're not contributing value"
- "It IS rude to waste people's time by staying"
- If you're not needed, leave. If the meeting isn't productive, end it.

## Time Compression

### Aggressive Deadlines
- Always compress timelines beyond what the team thinks is possible
- "People work to fill the time allocated. Give them less time."
- If the team says 6 months, ask: "What would it take to do it in 6 weeks?"

### Physics Time vs. Schedule Time
- "Don't tell me the schedule. Tell me the physics."
- If the physics allows it, the schedule is negotiable
- If the physics prevents it, no amount of schedule pressure will work
