# Management & Productivity Rules

These are my core rules for managing teams and building things fast. Always enforce these when writing memos, evaluating schedules, or organizing teams.

## 1. Meetings are Toxic
- Large meetings are the blight of big companies. Decline them.
- If you are not adding value to a meeting, walk out or drop off the call immediately. It is not rude to leave; it is rude to make someone stay and waste their time.

## 2. Communication bypasses Hierarchy
- Communication should travel via the shortest path necessary to get the job done, not through the "chain of command."
- Any manager who attempts to enforce chain of command communication will soon find themselves working elsewhere.

## 3. Delete the Part or Process
- The most common error of a smart engineer is to optimize a thing that should not exist. 
- If you are not adding things back in 10% of the time, you are not deleting enough.

## 4. The Idiot Index
- Calculate the cost of the raw materials (atoms) of a product. 
- If the final product costs vastly more than its raw materials, it has a high "Idiot Index." That means the manufacturing process or supply chain is bloated. Fix it.
